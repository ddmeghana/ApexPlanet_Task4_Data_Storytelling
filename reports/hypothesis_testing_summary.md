# Hypothesis Testing Summary

## Business Question
Does a higher discount reduce profitability?

## Hypothesis

**Null Hypothesis (H₀):** There is no significant difference in profit between low-discount and high-discount orders.

**Alternative Hypothesis (H₁):** High-discount orders have significantly lower profit than low-discount orders.

## Method Used
An independent two-sample t-test was performed to compare the average profit of low-discount and high-discount orders.

## Groups Compared
- Low Discount Orders: Discount ≤ 20%
- High Discount Orders: Discount > 20%

## Significance Level
α = 0.05

## Result
The p-value obtained from the t-test was less than 0.05.

## Interpretation
Since the p-value is less than 0.05, the null hypothesis is rejected. This indicates that there is a statistically significant difference in profit between low-discount and high-discount orders.

## Business Conclusion
High discounts significantly reduce profitability. The business should optimize discount strategies to protect profit margins.
